"Nguyen" or "Win" your battle.

At birth, twins are labeled as "A" or "B" on file. Nguyen was labeled as the "B" twin and born with schizophrenia, diagnosed by the age of 20. As a Vietnamese-American, the stigma surrounding mental illness is prevalent in the Asian community. Schizophrenia affects .3-.7 individuals and is only an uncommon disorder of the demographics, which include people with bipolar, depression, anxiety, etc. This brand is the culture that advocates for mental illness awareness; breaking stereotypes and stigma around mental illness. Nguyen, or rather, "win" your battle.

What is "Bệnh"?

The origin of "Bệnh" stems from the vietnamese word, "sick", hence the play-on word for "sick designs". This is a very important concept as a design that advocates for mental health awareness. Mental health is always unwavering and can sometimes be chronic. Depression, bi-polar, schizophrenia, etc. can have a damaging effect on someones health and well-being. Day-to-day tasks start to become reluctant. Motivation becomes discouraged. And finally, hopelessness settles in. "Bệnh" Designs' connotation of "sick" is not to be weak, but to own your life, Boldly, Beautifully, and Brilliantly. This design strives to protect and battle illness. "Sick" designs is not meant to harm or wish upon bad luck, but rather demonstrate that a person can achieve and triumph their battle with mental illness. The first step is to to "Biết".

What is "Biết"?

"Biết" broken down is "to know" and to "become aware". The first stage in mental illness is DENIAL. Stigma surrounding mental illness prevents people from speaking up about it...and when they do, people think it is seeking for attention or "normalizing" the situation. Outright downplaying the seriousness of someone's mental conditions! This is why it is important to "Biết" Expression, or rather "know expression". Speak up about it. Positively, rather than negatively, so that it doesn't become a trigger. For instance, talk about WHAT you're doing to effectively change. Become free. Become AWARE of your illness and use that as leverage to recovery. Educate yourself on mental illness. Educate others.
